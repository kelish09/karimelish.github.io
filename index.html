Short Bio:

Since 2016, I have been a faculty member in the Department of Computer Science at Florida Polytechnic University, where I currently serve as an Associate Professor. Prior to joining Florida Poly, I held a faculty appointment as an Assistant Professor of Computer Science at Purdue University Fort Wayne.

In 2018, I received the Florida Poly ABLAZE Award for Excellence in Teaching in recognition of exemplary pedagogical practices, outstanding teaching effectiveness inside and outside the classroom, and a demonstrated ability to inspire, promote, and sustain students’ intellectual development. The award also acknowledges contributions to course and program development and efforts to foster critical thinking among students.

I earned both my Ph.D. and M.S. degrees in Computer Science from Virginia Tech. I am a Senior Member of the ACM and IEEE.

Research Interests:

I am working in Software Security, Software Engineering, and Applied AI/ML, with a specific focus on:

  • Program analysis — for malware detection and mobile application security,
  • Software analytics and quality — including metrics, vulnerability detection, and automated assurance, and
  • AI, LLMs, and ML for software engineering — including secure code analysis, refactoring, and automated reasoning about software behavior.
